# Pet Solutions Alliance Corporation - Master Reference

**Date:** November 16, 2025  
**Status:** Design & Planning Phase  
**Purpose:** Comprehensive reference for all 12 divisions/companies within Pet Solutions Alliance

---

## Executive Summary

Pet Solutions Alliance Corporation is a comprehensive ecosystem of 12 integrated companies providing end-to-end pet care solutions. The alliance spans medical services, licensing, content creation, products, healthcare insurance, training, food, technology, grooming, care services, philanthropy, and real estate.

### Organizational Structure

```
Pet Solutions Alliance Corporation (Parent - Delaware C Corp)
├── 1. Acquisition CO (Medical/Wellness - Human Services)
├── 2. Licensure & Identification CO (Pet Registry & Certification)
├── 3. Content & Media CO (Entertainment & Marketing)
├── 4. Products CO (E-commerce & Custom Gear)
├── 5. Healthcare CO (Pet Insurance)
├── 6. Training CO (Pet & Human Training)
├── 7. Pet Food CO (Nutrition & Supplements)
├── 8. AI Plus Tech CO (Technology & Innovation)
├── 9. Grooming CO (Salon Services & Products)
├── 10. Care Services CO (Day Care, Boarding, Transport)
├── 11. Foundation (Philanthropic Programs)
└── 12. Real Estate CO (Properties & Facilities)
```

## Division Overview

### 1. Acquisition CO (Newburgh & Wellness Services, Inc.)

**Focus:** Human medical and wellness services for pet caregivers  
**Legal Structure:** MSO (Management Services Organization) with state entities

**Core Services:**
- Medical & adjunct services (flexible MSGRLLC model)
- Behavioral health & mental health support
- Prescription services
- Semaglutide Limited (housing clearance, bariatric surgery clearance, gender-affirming care)
- Telemedicine infrastructure
- Specialty evaluations

**State Entities:**
- STATE MSO LLCs: California, Texas, New York
- STATE PLLCs: California, Texas

**Key Differentiator:** Supports pet parents' well-being, recognizing that healthy caregivers = healthier pets

### Technology Stack

**Shared Infrastructure:**
- **Authentication:** Clerk (unified login across divisions)
- **Database:** PostgreSQL (multi-tenant schema per division)
- **API Layer:** tRPC (type-safe inter-service communication)
- **Event Bus:** Redis/NATS (cross-division events)
- **File Storage:** Cloudinary/S3 (media and documents)
- **Payment Processing:** Stripe (unified billing)
- **Search:** Algolia/Elasticsearch (unified search)

**Division-Specific:**
- **Blockchain:** Ethereum/Polygon (Licensure registry)
- **Video Streaming:** Mux/AWS MediaConvert (Content & Media)
- **AI/ML:** TensorFlow/PyTorch (AI Plus Tech, Food CO)
- **Geolocation:** Google Maps API (Care Services)
- **WebSockets:** Socket.io (Real-time tracking)

## Data Sharing Model

### Central Data Entities

**Pet Profile (Shared Across All Divisions):**
```typescript
interface PetProfile {
  id: string;
  name: string;
  species: 'dog' | 'cat' | 'other';
  breed: string[];
  birthDate: Date;
  weight: number;
  
  // Division-specific IDs
  licenseId?: string;        // Licensure CO
  insurancePolicyId?: string; // Healthcare CO
  registryId?: string;        // Licensure CO (blockchain)
  dnaResultId?: string;       // AI Plus Tech CO
  
  // Shared metadata
  ownerId: string;
  createdAt: Date;
  updatedAt: Date;
}
```

**Owner Profile (Shared):**
```typescript
interface OwnerProfile {
  id: string;
  clerkId: string;
  email: string;
  phone: string;
  name: string;
  
  // Cross-division access
  membershipLevel: 'free' | 'basic' | 'premium' | 'enterprise';
  subscriptions: DivisionSubscription[];
  
  // Privacy controls
  dataSharing: DivisionDataSharing;
}
```

## Risk Analysis

### Market Risks

**Competition:**
- Established pet care brands (Petco, PetSmart, Chewy)
- Insurance providers (Trupanion, Nationwide)
- Tech disruptors (Rover, Wag)

**Mitigation:** Integrated ecosystem creates switching costs

**Regulatory:**
- State licensing requirements (Licensure)
- Insurance regulations (Healthcare)
- Medical services compliance (Acquisition)

**Mitigation:** Legal team per division, compliance-first approach

### Operational Risks

**Technology:**
- System integration complexity
- Data security and privacy
- Scalability challenges

**Mitigation:** Phased rollout, proven tech stack, security audits

**Execution:**
- Team scaling (5→50+ employees)
- Multi-division coordination
- Quality control

**Mitigation:** Strong leadership, clear governance, division autonomy
